    About this app

    A local chess workbench for playing against Stockfish and analyzing any position. See the top engine moves, step through the principal variation, and get plain-English explanations of the ideas behind each move.

    The short move blurbs are assembled from plain chess heuristics (material, checks, forks, and the like). Stockfish and the rest of the app run on your machine.

    What each tab does

    • Play — play a full game against Stockfish at a strength you choose.
    • Analyze — paste a FEN or load your current game, and review the engine's top candidate moves with explanations.
    • Review — paste a PGN and get per-side feedback across the whole game: accuracy, recurring issues, and your worst moments.

    Reading the Analyze results

    Top moves
    The moves Stockfish considers strongest in this position, ranked best-first.
    Eval
    How good the resulting position is for the side to move, measured in pawns. +1.20 ≈ up a pawn and change; +M3 means mate in 3.
    Principal variation (PV)
    Stockfish's expected best line after the move. Click any move in the line to jump the board to that position.
    Why this move
    A short summary of the move's main ideas and drawbacks, built from patterns: checks, captures, forks, development, king safety, and missed chances.

    The explainer is best for concrete ideas (checks, forks, material, development). For tricky strategic positions, trust the Eval and PV most — those come straight from Stockfish.

    How it works (for the curious)

    Engine strength

    In Play, the Limit ~Elo toggle approximates a human rating with Stockfish's own strength limits (1320-3170). Stockfish's calibrated range starts around ~1320 Elo, so the slider clamps to the actual supported range.

    In Analyze, the Depth preset trades speed for accuracy (Fast is quick and shallow; Extreme is slow and strong).

    Quality labels

    Each candidate is bucketed by how much evaluation it loses versus the best move: best, excellent, good, playable, inaccuracy, mistake, blunder. The "−N cp vs best" chip shows the centipawn loss.

    The explainer

    The "Why this move" panel runs a set of deterministic checks on the board before and after the move — it looks for things like checks, forks, captures with good exchange value, castling, passed pawns, opened lines, hanging pieces, and weakened king safety. Misses … bullets appear when the best move had a tactic (mate, winning capture, fork) that your move didn't.

    Where the numbers come from

    Analysis comes from Stockfish, and the move explanations come from deterministic chess rules that inspect the position before and after each move.
